How is the Risk Score calculated?

The Risk Score (0–100) combines multiple real-world data sources to estimate overall vehicle reliability risk. It factors in owner-reported complaints from NHTSA, manufacturer-acknowledged repair bulletins (TSBs), any open government safety investigations, dealer service advisories, and the brand's overall quality rating. Higher mileage vehicles receive an additional wear adjustment to reflect accumulated risk. A lower score means fewer reported problems and better expected reliability.
