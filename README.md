## What Does CarAdvisr Do?

When you're buying a used car, dealers won't tell you about hidden safety issues. CarAdvisr pulls real government safety data and cross-references it with recalls, investigations, and manufacturer bulletins to build a complete risk profile for any vehicle — all in about 30 seconds.

**Your mileage changes everything.** A 2018 Toyota Camry at 30,000 miles is a very different car than the same Camry at 120,000 miles. CarAdvisr doesn't just look at the make and model — it analyzes complaint patterns at your specific mileage to predict which problems you're most likely to face *right now*. The risk score, inspection checklist, and AI verdict all shift based on where the car is in its lifecycle.

**AI powered by real data, not guesswork.** Our AI doesn't make things up. Every recommendation, verdict, and inspection item is grounded in real complaint data reported by actual vehicle owners to the federal government. The AI reads thousands of data points — complaint counts, failure patterns, recall severity, investigation outcomes — and translates them into plain-English guidance that anyone can understand. No hallucinated stats. No generic advice. Just real data made actionable.

Unlike Carfax, which shows what *already happened* to a specific car (accidents, ownership, service), CarAdvisr shows what is *likely to go wrong* based on data from every owner who has ever reported a problem with that exact make, model, and year — at your exact mileage.

## What's in a Report?

Every report is free and includes:

- **Mileage-Tailored Risk Score (0–100)** — Not a generic rating. This score shifts based on your car's exact mileage, combining complaint severity, recall history, technical service bulletins, NHTSA investigations, and brand reliability data. A car at 40k miles will score differently than the same car at 110k miles — because the risks are different. Lower is better.

- **AI Buyer's Verdict** — A clear "Buy", "Consider", or "Walk Away" recommendation written by AI that has analyzed every data point in the report. The reasoning cites specific complaint patterns, recall severity, and mileage risk — never generic filler.

- **Mileage-Specific Inspection Checklist** — AI-generated from real complaint data, not a generic car-buying checklist. Tells you exactly what to check on *this* car at *this* mileage — what to look for, red flags that mean "walk away", questions to ask the seller, and estimated repair costs if something is wrong.

- **Complaint Breakdown** — See how many owners have reported problems, which systems are most affected (engine, transmission, brakes, etc.), and how complaint volume trends over time.

- **Recall & Safety Alerts** — Every open and closed recall with campaign details, consequences, and the manufacturer's official fix.

- **Market Price Analysis** — See if the asking price is fair based on real listing data from thousands of comparable vehicles.

- **Risk Breakdown** — A detailed report card showing how the vehicle scores across different risk categories, with a radar chart visualization.

## How Is This Different from Carfax?

| Feature | CarAdvisr | Carfax |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Price | Free | $40–$100 |
| What it shows | Risk prediction (what's *likely* to go wrong) | History report (what *already* happened) |
| Data source | NHTSA complaints, recalls, investigations, TSBs, manufacturer bulletins | Title records, service history, accident reports |
| AI analysis | Yes — buyer's verdict + inspection checklist | No |
| Mileage-specific | Yes — tailored to your exact mileage | No |
| Requires VIN | No — just make, model, year | Yes |

They complement each other. Carfax tells you about *that specific car's* past. CarAdvisr tells you about *that type of car's* risk profile. Use both for the full picture.

## Data Sources

CarAdvisr analyzes data from:

- **NHTSA (National Highway Traffic Safety Administration)** — 2.18 million+ owner complaints, safety ratings, and recall records from the U.S. government's vehicle safety database
- **Technical Service Bulletins (TSBs)** — Manufacturer-acknowledged issues and recommended fixes
- **NHTSA Investigations** — Federal safety investigations into vehicle defects
- **Manufacturer Communications** — Service advisories issued by automakers to dealerships
- **MarketCheck** — Real-time vehicle listing data for market price analysis
